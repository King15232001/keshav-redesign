Academic Calendar

Academic planning page for session activities, teaching-learning schedule, examination preparation, student events and institutional updates.

Academic Planning

Structured Academic Year for Students

The academic calendar helps students, faculty members and parents understand the overall academic flow of the session. It provides direction for admissions, teaching-learning, internal activities, examinations, student support and institutional events.

Students should regularly follow the academic calendar along with syllabus, examination, result and notice-related information so that they remain prepared throughout the session.

Academic Calendar Sections

Key Areas of Academic Planning

The academic calendar is not only a date sheet. It is a planning tool for students and the institution.

01

Admission Phase

Admission process, document guidance, programme selection and student registration support.

02

Orientation

Initial academic guidance for students to understand college rules, programmes and support services.

03

Teaching-Learning

Regular classes, syllabus coverage, subject guidance and academic progress monitoring.

04

Internal Activities

Class tests, assignments, practical work, seminars, workshops and student participation activities.

05

Student Support

Syllabus, examination, result, library, laboratories, ICT and academic assistance.

06

Quality Initiatives

IQAC activities, feedback, institutional documentation and academic improvement processes.

07

Examination

Exam preparation, schedule awareness, internal readiness and result-related support.

08

Co-Curricular Activities

NSS, skill development, seminars, workshops, gallery activities and student engagement.

Tentative Academic Flow

Session Planning Overview

This section can be updated with exact official dates whenever the college finalizes the academic calendar PDF or notice.

Phase 01 Admission

Admission, Counselling and Document Verification

Students review programme details, fill the admission form, prepare documents and contact the college office for confirmation and support.

Phase 02 Orientation

Student Orientation and Academic Introduction

Students are introduced to college rules, academic programmes, departments, syllabus, facilities, examination system and student support sections.

Phase 03 Learning

Regular Classes and Syllabus Coverage

Faculty members conduct regular teaching-learning activities and students follow syllabus, classroom instructions and academic calendar updates.

Phase 04 Activities

Seminars, Workshops and Student Development

Students participate in academic, co-curricular, NSS, skill development and campus activities for overall development.

Phase 05 Assessment

Internal Preparation and Examination Readiness

Students prepare for examinations through syllabus revision, faculty guidance, assignments, practical work and academic support.

Phase 06 Result

Examination, Result and Academic Progress

Students follow examination notices, result updates and next academic instructions through the student zone and college communication channels.

Student Guidance for Academic Calendar

Students should use the academic calendar as a planning document throughout the academic session. It helps them stay prepared for classes, assignments, practical work, examinations and student activities.

A
Track Important Dates

Students should regularly check important dates related to admission, classes, examinations and student activities.

B
Follow the Syllabus

Use the syllabus page with the academic calendar to plan study targets and examination preparation.

C
Prepare for Examination

Students should plan revision, practical work, assignments and examination preparation according to the academic flow.

D
Participate in Activities

Academic and co-curricular activities help students develop confidence, leadership and communication skills.
