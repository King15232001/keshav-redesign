Study Planning

How Students Should Use the Syllabus

A syllabus is the main academic reference for every student. It should be used for regular study, assignments, practical work and examination preparation.

01

Understand Course Structure

Check subjects, papers, units and learning areas before beginning regular study.

02

Plan Weekly Study

Divide syllabus topics weekly with the help of academic calendar and class guidance.

03

Prepare Notes

Maintain subject-wise notes, important questions and revision points for every unit.

04

Use Library Resources

Use library books and reference material for deeper understanding of syllabus topics.

05

Complete Assignments

Assignments and practical work should be completed according to syllabus requirements.

06

Follow Faculty Guidance

Discuss syllabus doubts with faculty members and attend classes regularly.

07

Revise Before Exams

Use syllabus units to revise systematically before internal and final examinations.

08

Track Progress

Mark completed topics and identify pending areas for focused preparation.

Syllabus and Examination Preparation

Students should connect syllabus preparation with the examination page and academic calendar. This helps in better time management, revision planning and academic performance.

A
Read the Complete Syllabus

Do not study only selected topics. Read the complete syllabus and understand the course structure properly.

B
Connect with Academic Calendar

Use the academic calendar to manage study schedule, internal work and examination preparation.

C
Check Examination Updates

Follow the examination page for notices, instructions and exam-related information.

D
Ask for Academic Support

Contact faculty members or the college office if syllabus or examination-related clarification is required.
